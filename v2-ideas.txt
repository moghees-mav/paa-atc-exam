The examinee, once served with the final result, should not be able to re-take the exam (mode 2 only).

- Authentication procedures specifically for Mode 2. Regulatory requirements dictate that candidates undergoing testing must be authenticated. Currently, during manual testing, the HQ appointed supervisors make sure each candidate who sits the exam is actually them and not imposter through request for CNIC and ATC License. They can then determine authenticity through these 2 picture-carrying documents. Initially, for mode 2, once an examinee enters their service number and their details are fetched, they can then be asked to enter their license number and cnic number for validation against RTL db. Later on, we might implement camera snapshots before, during and after exam to authenticate with facial recognition, and/or fingerprint authentication against RTL-db. or some other identification check, whicever is most effective/convenient. Since this exam is served by a highly trained and controlled group of people in controlled setup, this is simply  a regulatory requirement, not a huuge priority right now.

- another regulatory requirement, not really relevant here but worth considering, is to have detailed reasoning and linkage to official SOP docs and manuals regarding inclusion, or lack thereof, of certain documents in mode 2 testing. Might be good to keep such information for ready reference in case an examinee requests why they got certain questions.

note 1: ATC personnel go through regular scheduled testing to serve regulatory and training requirements. Some exams are small, while others more significant. The most significant testing occurs during Categorisation Examinations, conducted annually through Headquarters deployed supervisors. The Cat exam is broken down into Non-Radar and Radar groups. ATS Personnel who have not undergone Radar training will only give the Non-radar exam, whereas ATS personnel who have undergone radar training, must give Non-radar exam as well as Radar exam. The RTL-db api will allow for automatic determination of which examinees are to be given the additional radar exam based on their training qualification data.

note 2: An ATCO goes through regualar scheduled testing. Some is done by HQ (Like categorisation examination annually) while some is done by the location (pre-solo checks), and some is requested by On-Job Training Instructor (OJTI) during location ATS-unit specific testing.
